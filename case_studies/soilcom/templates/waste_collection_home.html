Soilcom Waste Collections

This project aims at building an interactive map of the biowaste collection in Europe. The waste streams are characterised based on the collection system and the materials that are allowed to be given into the collection. The main source are information given by the responsible waste collectors to the local citizens in their respective catchment.

View map
Collectors

The collector is the organisation that is responsible for providing the means of biowaste collection, collecting the waste and managing its utilisation.

View collectors
Catchments

A catchment is the area that a collection is dedicated to. Usually they are administrative regions, such as counties.

View catchments
Collection systems

The collection systems describe the means by which the collectors organise the collection. It is determined by how and when and where the collector is active and provides space and containers for the biowaste.

View collection systems
Waste streams

Waste streams are determined by the materials that are included in the collection. While rough categories, such as "green waste" might be shared among the collectors, the details differ regionally and play a significant role in how the respective waste stream is composed and can be utilised in the end.

View waste streams
Waste categories

Categories allow to order waste streams into similar groups.

View waste categories
Collection frequencies

The frequencies of the collections can be grouped into categories. These can be created or edited here.

View collection frequencies
Waste components

Waste components are materials that are present in the waste.

View waste components
Collections

Each waste collection is conducted by a specific collector in a specific catchment and for only one waste stream. More than one collection per collector or catchment is possible.
